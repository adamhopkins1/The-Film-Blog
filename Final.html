3 Films to Watch for at Venice/Telluride/Toronto Film Festival

These are the 3 top films to keep an eye for in the running for Golden Lion/Telluride Film Prize/Toronto Audience Award.(Updated as of 9/6/2022).

Venice Film Festival Predictions

Tar(2022) Tar ( dir:Todd Fields)

We Project The Critically Praised Feature from Feature will win the Golden Lion by the Venice Jury and Best Actress For Cate Blanchett for her performance the director prize could go to Todd Field's but that is a prediction as there are more films to fortunately view.(Update:Laura Poiras "All The Beauty and The Bloodshed" Won the Golden Lion as of 09/10/2022

The Banshees of Inisherin(2022) The Banshees of Inisherin ( dir:Michael Mcdounough)

We Project that Banshees of Inisherin will be awarded at venice for Best screenplay and Colin Farell could recieve mentions for his Supporting or acting lead Role as the forgiving friend opposite Brendan Gleeson.This will be another win for Mcdounough whose has recieved praise for his screenwriting in his past works.

All The Beauty and the Bloodshed(2022) All The Beauty And The Bloodshed ( dir:Laura Poitras)

We Project All The Beauty and The Bloodshed Will Be in contention for the Golden Lion and will win in the Venice Documentary Section the fact the film will screen at all 4 major festivals (Venice, Telluride, Toronto and NYFF) will position Neon and Laura Poitras for a campaign that will lead them to possibly be a frontrunner for best documentary and best picture nominee at the 2023 Academy Awards.

Telluride Film Predictions

Women Talking(2022) Tar ( dir:Sarah Polley)

Since Telluride is more of a festival that celebrates Cinema this will be more word of mouth buzz leading to the Toronto Film Festival based on the screenings from critics Women Talking will generate buzz from critic viewers based on Sarah Polley's direction and either Rooney Mara's or Jessie Buckley's performance. Women Talking could be in consideration for best direction and best picture alongside a adapted screenplay buzz also. Toronto Film Festival Predictions

The Fabelmans(2022) The Fabelmans( dir:Steven Speilberg)

Premiering at the Toronto International Film Festival on September 10th Steven Spielberg's anticipated Autobiograph Feature about his formative years in Arizona in his Teens and what made him along his journey to make film and direct features along the way.The film was written by Spielberg and Tony Kushner and Many are anticipating the film which features perfomances from Michelle Willams as the character's mother who helped inspire his creativity alongside Paul Dano as his father and Seth Rogan as his uncle alongside a tour-de-force supporting role in Judd Hirsch The Fabelmans explores the past to mine questions and to give a backstory to Americas Formost Greatest Filmakers. In retrospect I can see this depending on the audience awards love and inspiration of spielberg playing into the Toronto Audience to be a major hit with the TIFF crowd. The question however is will this film have spielberg in the conversation well that depends but its possible.

5 min
The Greatest Beer Run Ever(2022) Tar ( dir:Peter Farelly)

The Greatest Beer Run Ever which is set to be released this November on Apple TV plus is from the Director of the 2018 film "Green Book" Which went on to previously win the Audience Award at the 2017 Toronto Film Festival in the film which switches settings from a civil-rights era south to a Vietnam-war setting with an absurd premise is lead by Zac Efron and Russel Crowe Zac plays an aimless purposeless character who gets inspired to deliver Beer to his friends who are fighting in the Vietnam War. The feature has potential to play to the Toronto Film Crowd and to the male voting block of attendees who are Academy Members Could this and Causeway starring Jennifer Lawrence be Apple big players this fall? Lets not forget this could be a year of excellent films such as Babylon(more on that later) and Other strong players set to release within the timeframe.And lest we forget Apple's last best picture winner was last years CODA which got its premier at last years Sundance Film Festival.

3 min
Babylon(2022) Babylon ( dir:Damien Chazelle)

Finally the biggest picuture possibly of the TIFF will be Babylon which the Tiff audiences will recieve early first look footage of alongside a Q&A session with Director Damien Chazelle. This will be pivotal not only for the filmaker and the season overall but for the distributor Paramount Pictures who is currently in Freefall financially and creatively. If Babylon does the impossible and saves Paramount Pictures from Chapter 11 this will be a moumental moment in the studios history but alongside it Babylon may have the Cineaste effect the same way Top Gun:Maverick had on the Blockbuster film audience. Babylon is an upcoming film written directed produced by Damien Chazelle whose last film "La La Land" earned him a Best Director Oscar and his co-star Emma Stone her first Best Actress Oscar. This is a huge film that is said to be over 3hrs in length and has been compared to Fellini's Satyricon and F.Scott Fitzgerald's The Great Gatsby on steroids.It stars Brad Pitt and Margot Robbie as actors transitioning from the silent era of film of the 20's to the Talkies of the 30's it features a huge ensemble cast that will make it easily a Sag Frontrunner. So this is the major film not only for the TIFF crowd but for everyone in the orbit of Cinema, Film and Movies overall.
